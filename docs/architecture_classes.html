Architecture class guide

Architecture labels are interpretation labels. They summarize record distribution and payload/context burden.

LabelHow to read it
Low-record-burden interpretation-limitedComputed, but record support is sparse.
Compact downstream-CDS record-coverage hypothesisHigh record coverage may be obtained from a transcript boundary; read with donor payload and context caveats.
Compact local exon/block record clusterRecord burden is concentrated in a local exon or adjacent exon block.
Downstream-CDS high-coverage with context burdenDownstream coverage is high, but boundary, splice, dosage, or context assumptions dominate interpretation.
Large-payload / donor-burden stressHigh record coverage may require a large payload or heavy delivery/evidence burden.
Tumor-predisposition control/cautionHigh ClinVar burden is retained as a control/caution label, not suitability.
Mapping/locus-complex cautionInterpretation is constrained by locus or mapping complexity.
Single-exon record clusterA single exon explains a notable fraction of record burden.
Diffuse/moderate record architectureNo strong compact unit dominates record coverage.
Mechanism-complex limitationRepeat, noncoding, splicing, or mechanism complexity can exceed VCF-based architecture metrics.
PrimeReplace Atlas is a pre-implementation transcript-architecture resource. Record coverage is not patient coverage.
